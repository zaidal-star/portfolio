Copilot Prompt Library

What it is: A library of generative AI prompts you can adapt to expedite your work—grouped by common use cases and paired with guardrails to protect sensitive information.

Who it’s for: Anyone using AI assistants (such as Copilot) to summarize documents, draft SOPs, rewrite emails, manage risk logs, track studies, or create training content.

Problems it solves

  • Starting from a blank page when summarizing or drafting can be time‑consuming.
  • Lack of awareness about safety and privacy when using AI assistants.
  • Inconsistent language across prompts and teams.

What’s included

  • 15+ prompts grouped by use case: summarizing documents, meeting notes, drafting SOPs, email rewrites, risk/issue logs, study trackers, and training content.
  • Guardrails and a “do not paste” list for each group (PHI/PII, secrets, unpublished results, internal identifiers).
  • A red‑flag checklist to help you spot and remove sensitive details.
  • Sanitize‑first steps to prepare your material before sending it to an AI assistant.

How to use

  1. Identify your use case (e.g., summarizing a document, rewriting an email).
  2. Select a prompt from the corresponding category and adjust the wording to fit your context.
  3. Before copying text into the AI assistant, run through the red‑flag checklist and remove or anonymize sensitive information.
  4. Provide only the necessary context to the assistant and review the output critically.
  5. Incorporate the AI‑generated draft into your workflow and update the prompt library as you discover improvements.
Safety & Responsible Use:
  • Never share personal health information, personally identifiable information, secrets, unpublished results, or internal identifiers with AI systems.
  • Redact names, participant IDs, or proprietary methods before submitting your prompt.
  • If a question seems sensitive, consult your governance policies or supervisor before using AI.

Prompt Categories (preview)

Summarizing Documents

  • “Summarize the key findings of this research paper in three bullet points.”
  • “Provide a concise abstract of the following report, highlighting the goals, methods, results, and conclusions.”
  • “List the strengths and weaknesses identified in this study.”

Meeting Notes

  • “Summarize the key decisions and action items from this meeting transcript.”
  • “Identify open questions and follow‑up tasks from these meeting notes.”

Drafting SOPs

  • “Create a draft Standard Operating Procedure for data backup based on these guidelines.”
  • “Outline a set of steps to ensure reproducible analysis in a research workflow.”

Email Rewrites

  • “Rewrite this email to be more concise and professional.”
  • “Draft a polite reminder email to a colleague about an upcoming deadline.”

Risk/Issue Logs

  • “List potential risks and mitigation strategies for this project.”
  • “Create an issue log entry summarizing a data quality problem and proposing a resolution.”

Study Trackers

  • “Generate a progress update template for a longitudinal study.”
  • “Draft a study tracker that records milestones, dates, and responsible parties.”

Training Content

  • “Outline a beginner‑level training session on using AI in documentation workflows.”
  • “Create an agenda for an intermediate workshop on AI‑assisted data analysis.”
