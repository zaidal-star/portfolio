Planner Workflow Template

What it is: A structured board template for managing projects in Microsoft Planner or any Kanban‑style tool. It provides a consistent layout for tasks, naming conventions, status tags, and review cycles.

Who it’s for: Project managers, team leads, researchers, or anyone who wants to manage tasks consistently across projects.

Problems it solves

  • Inconsistent task tracking across teams.
  • Lack of clarity about roles and ownership.
  • Sloppy hand‑offs due to missing steps.
  • Weekly reviews falling through the cracks.

What’s included

  • Boards/buckets structure: Backlog, In Progress, Needs Review, Completed.
  • Task naming convention: YYYY‑MM‑DD_Project_TaskName for easy sorting and searching.
  • Status tags: Not Started, Active, Blocked, Needs Review, Completed.
  • Weekly review cadence: Guidance on how to run short weekly check‑ins to update statuses and unblock tasks.
  • Example project: A generic research study board showing how to break down phases (hypothesis, data collection, analysis, reporting).
  • Handoff checklist: A list of checks to perform before moving a task to “Completed” (documentation updated, data archived, stakeholders notified).
  • RACI‑lite roles: A matrix to assign Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed roles for each task.

How to use

  1. Make a copy of the template board in your project management tool.
  2. Rename buckets to align with your workflow if necessary.
  3. Create tasks using the naming convention (e.g., 2025‑12‑15_Study_DataCollection).
  4. Assign tasks to team members and tag them with appropriate status labels.
  5. Schedule a weekly review meeting to update statuses and discuss blockers.
  6. Use the handoff checklist when moving a task from “Needs Review” to “Completed.”
Safety & Responsible Use: Do not include confidential or sensitive information (PHI/PII, unpublished results, internal codes) in task titles or descriptions. Use generic descriptors where appropriate and store sensitive details securely elsewhere.
